Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

What should I bring when getting admitted?
+
Bring your National ID, insurance card, list of current medications, medical history, doctor referral (if applicable), and personal essentials like toiletries and a change of clothes.
How does the admission process work?
+
Once a doctor refers you or you arrive via emergency, you'll go through an intake process that includes registration, initial assessment by a nurse, and room/cabin assignment based on your condition.
Can I choose my doctor or specialist?
+
In emergency situations, doctors are assigned based on availability. For planned admissions, you can request a specific doctor if they are affiliated with our hospital and available during your visit.
How are tests and diagnostic procedures scheduled?
+
Tests are usually ordered by your treating doctor and coordinated by hospital staff. Priority is given based on urgency. You will be informed of your scheduled time and any preparation needed.
Do you offer 24/7 emergency services?
+
Yes, Green City Hospital provides round-the-clock emergency services. Our emergency department is staffed at all times to handle life-threatening and urgent medical situations.
